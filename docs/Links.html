Current trends

Overall GHG emissions

Between 2016 and 2020 GHG emission from canton Sarajevo grew at a rate of about 2.1% a year - see Figure 1 grey bars. The dip in 2020 motivated by COVID-19 lock-down is likely to be short-lived and a rebound on emissions in 2021 and 2022 is expected in line with global trends. Assuming a relative rebound of emissions for 2021 similar to that observed for the EU implies an estimate of about 2300 Thousand tons CO2eq for canton Sarajevo - see Figure 1 blue bars. Such a development would further place the current emission trajectory at odds with that necessary for a 80% cut in emission by 2050 - see Figure 1 grey bars. On a per-capita basis the emissions in canton Sarajevo are estimated at circa 5.1 tons CO2eq for the year 2021. Details about the methodology, scope and data sources used can be found in the GHG emission inventory tab.

Sector decomposition

In terms of sector breakdown, residential buildings and road transport dominate the share of total emissions in the canton - see Figure 2. Combined, these two sectors were associated with over 60% of total emissions in the year 2020. Non-residential buildings associated with services and commerce make up circa 13% of emissions while the industry sector is associated with 15% of total GHG emissions. Emissions from electricity are accounted in each sector respectively. How sector-specific emissions evolved post-pandemic is unclear but judging from other monitoring services transport, power and industry should lead the way. Note that the sector breakdown presented in Figure 2 tries to be as close as possible to that required by the Covenant of Mayors (CoM) but other allocations are possible.

Figure 2 - GHG emissions in the year 2020

Sectors exhibit marked differences regarding past trends of GHG emissions - see Table 1. Disregarding the year 2020 due to the economic and behavior distortions introduced by the global pandemic, one can observe a sharp increase of yearly emissions from the household sector (including electricity and district heating) in the order of 4.5%/year. Road transportation has increased about 1.6%/year between 2016 and 2019 while the increase in municipal services (which includes gas consumption in municipal buildings and public lighting) has been moderate and below 1%/year.
